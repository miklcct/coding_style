Definitions of naming rules

The original casing is always discarded in all naming rules. For example, HTTP Server becomes HttpServer for PascalCase and httpServer for camelCase. Similarly, supports IPv6 on iOS becomes supportsIpv6OnIos for camelCase.

snake_case

snake_case is defined as writing words in all lower case, with a underscore separating words.

SCREAMING_SNAKE_CASE

SCREAMING_SNAKE_CASE is defined as writing words in all upper case, with a underscore separating words.

kebab-case

kebab-case is defined as writing words in all lower case, with a hyphen separating words.

camelCase

camelCase is defined as writing words without separators, in upper case for the first letter in the second and subsequent words, and lower case in all other letters.

PascalCase

PascalCase is defined as writing words without separators, in upper case for the first letter in all words, and lower case in all other letters.
